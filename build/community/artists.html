Christopher S. Daniel

Treemark illustrator and taproom table fabricator

Christopher S. Daniel is a sculptor working and living in Cincinnati, Ohio. Christopher owns and operates Blue Hell Studio, a steel fabrication and blacksmithing business, creating one of a kind, unique ornamental and functional steel work. Blue Hell Studio is the only blacksmith teaching facility in Cincinnati. Christopher received his Bachelor of Fine Arts from West Virginia University in 1994, and received his Master of Fine Arts in 1997 from the University of Cincinnati’s School of Design, Architecture, Art and Planning. His career over the last 16 years has included working for the Cincinnati Art Museum as the Lead Art Handler of the Decorative Arts De-installation Project, teaching as an Adjunct Professor at the Art Academy of Cincinnati, working as a freelance Toy Sculptor and illustrator and for two years he served as the Executive Director of The Mockbee, Inc., Ohio’s largest not for profit gallery.

Tom Post

Happy Amber and The Great Pumpcan illustrator

Growing up in Ohio, Tom can remember being excited about drawing as far back as age five. A freelance illustrator with a BFA in Fine Arts from Columbus College of Art and Design, he has a knack for creating unique, inspirational art for the project at hand. He has done advertising work for a variety of corporate clients and when time permits he pulls out his favorite medium, oil paints. Tom has been pursuing oil painting and expanding his teaching activities since 2001. He specializes in portraiture, figurative and painting en plein air. Tom is a member of the Cincinnati Art Club and his paintings are in the personal collection of various notable collectors. His works have been shown at The Cincinnati Nature Center, Homerama and can currently be seen at The Raymond Gallery in Hyde Park, Ohio.

Sprye illustrator

His subject matter tends to vary due to the various projects he works on but trees, robots, and video games are his most common reoccurring themes. Tony Gilardi

Identity Crisis illustrator

In 1996, at the College of Mount St. Joseph, I completed a BFA in Graphic Design. Also at Mount St. Joseph, in 2001, I completed an MA in Art Education. Since 1997 I have taught Art, both at the middle-grades level and currently at the senior high level, in the Northwest Local School district. I’ve stayed active creating and exhibiting artwork in galleries and venues throughout the Cincinnati area, and have had a chance to design a logo or two along the way. My work ranges from high-value graphite drawings to mixed-media abstracts. Nature, and in particular trees, seem to be my principal inspirations. Currently I live in West Harrison, IN, with my wife. I’ve been a homebrewer for 15 years, and have grown my own hops for the last 8 years. I heartily recommend homebrewing and hop-growing to all.
